Once upon a time, a little girl named Goldilocks went for a walk in the forest.
She came upon a cottage belonging to three bears: Papa Bear, Mama Bear, and Baby Bear.

The bears had left three bowls of porridge on the table to cool.
Goldilocks tasted Papa Bear's porridge — it was too hot.
She tasted Mama Bear's porridge — it was too cold.
She tasted Baby Bear's porridge — it was just right, and she ate it all up.

Next, Goldilocks tried the chairs. Papa Bear's chair was too hard, Mama Bear's too soft,
but Baby Bear's chair was just right — and she sat in it until it broke.

Tired, she went upstairs and tried the beds. Papa Bear's bed was too hard,
Mama Bear's too soft, but Baby Bear's bed was just right, and she fell fast asleep.

When the three bears came home they found their porridge eaten, their chairs disturbed,
and Goldilocks sleeping in Baby Bear's bed. She woke up, saw the bears, and ran away.
