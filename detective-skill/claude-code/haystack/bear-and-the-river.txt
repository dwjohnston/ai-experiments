High in the mountains lived a great brown bear named Kodiak. Every autumn he would
make the long journey down to the river where the salmon ran thick and silver.

Kodiak was the strongest bear in the valley, but he was also patient. He would stand
perfectly still in the shallows for hours, watching the water, waiting for the right moment.
The younger bears grew frustrated and splashed about, scaring the fish away.

One autumn, the salmon were late. The other bears grew thin and restless. Kodiak waited.
On the last day before the first snow, the salmon came — a great silver flood of them —
and Kodiak ate his fill while the impatient bears had already gone to find other food.

He returned to his cave on the mountainside, fat and ready for winter, dreaming of rivers.
